Switch from $t$ to $\sigma$

Two schedules
$X_t=\alpha_t X+\beta_t Z$
One ratio, one rescale
$\sigma_t=\dfrac{\beta_t}{\alpha_t}$
$X_\sigma=\dfrac{X_t}{\alpha_t}=X+\sigma Z$
$t=0 \;\longrightarrow\; \sigma=\infty$
pure noise
$t=1 \;\longrightarrow\; \sigma=0$
clean data

$\sigma$ is the actual amount of noise; two schedules collapse into one interpretable parameter.

Keep it brief: two knobs are awkward; one ratio is clear. Forward noising increases $\sigma$; reverse sampling drops it from $\sigma_{\max}$ to $0$.
