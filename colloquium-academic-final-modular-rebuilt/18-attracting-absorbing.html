Attracting & absorbing

Sampling is $\dfrac{dx_\sigma}{d\sigma}=-\dfrac{1}{\sigma}\bigl(m_\sigma(x_\sigma)-x_\sigma\bigr)$. Pick a set $\Omega$. How is the denoiser vector $m_\sigma(x)-x$ positioned relative to $\Omega$?

Every later theorem is a refined version of these same two ideas, for different choices of $\Omega$: the mean, a cluster, the support itself.

Keep the cartoons schematic. Attracting: two clearly separated vectors from the same start point, with clean LaTeX labels for \(x\), \(\operatorname{proj}_\Omega(x)\), and \(m_\sigma(x)\). Absorbing: a tube with inward-pointing boundary vectors, particle trapped.
