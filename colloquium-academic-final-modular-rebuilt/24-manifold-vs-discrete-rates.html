The rate depends on the data geometry

Same FM ODE, different endpoint geometry. How fast does $\Psi_\sigma(z)$ approach $\Psi_1(z)$?

Manifold support $M\subset\R^d$: $m$-dimensional, positive reach, bounded second fundamental form; $p$ has a smooth nonvanishing density on $M$. $$\|\Psi_1(z)-\Psi_\sigma(z)\|=O(\sqrt{\sigma})$$
Discrete support $p=\sum_{i=1}^N a_i\,\delta_{x_i}$. $$\|\Psi_1(z)-\Psi_\sigma(z)\|=O(\sigma)$$
The paper is not just proving existence. It already distinguishes different terminal behaviors depending on the support geometry — the manifold case converges at rate $\sqrt{\sigma}$, the discrete case at the faster rate $\sigma$.
