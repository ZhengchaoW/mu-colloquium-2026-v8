Why does this happen?

On empirical data, the denoiser loss itself already pushes toward a soft nearest-neighbor rule.

Recall — denoiser training loss $$\min_\theta\;\E_{\sigma,X,Z}\,\bigl\|m_\sigma^\theta(X+\sigma Z)-X\bigr\|^2.$$
Unique closed-form optimum on discrete $p$ $$ \begin{aligned} m_\sigma(x) &= \sum_{i=1}^{N}\frac{e^{-\|x-x_i\|^2/(2\sigma^2)}}{\sum_{j=1}^{N} e^{-\|x-x_j\|^2/(2\sigma^2)}}\, x_i \\ &\xrightarrow{\sigma\to 0}\; \arg\min_{x_i}\|x-x_i\|. \end{aligned} $$
As $\sigma\to 0$, the softmax weights collapse onto the nearest observation.

This is the mechanism behind the discrete decoder: the denoiser becomes nearest-neighbor in the terminal regime.
