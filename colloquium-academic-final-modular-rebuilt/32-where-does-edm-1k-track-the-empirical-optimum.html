What should have happened?

Finite-data training loss $$\min_\theta\;\E_{\sigma,X,Z}\,\bigl\|m_\sigma^\theta(X+\sigma Z)-X\bigr\|^2$$ On a finite dataset, the natural benchmark is the empirical-optimal denoiser.
Empirical optimum = weighted average $$m_\sigma(x)=\sum_{i=1}^N w_i(x,\sigma)\,x_i$$ $$w_i(x,\sigma)=\frac{\exp\!\left(-\|x-x_i\|^2/(2\sigma^2)\right)}{\sum_{j=1}^N \exp\!\left(-\|x-x_j\|^2/(2\sigma^2)\right)}$$ The top-right curve tracks $W_\sigma(x)=\max_i w_i(x,\sigma)$.
What is missing? Apparently, the memorizing model is not similar to the empirical optimal at small $\sigma$
A moment of productive confusion: the empirical optimum seems to suggest that small noise should already look lookup-like, but the experiments say otherwise. The next three slides explain why.
