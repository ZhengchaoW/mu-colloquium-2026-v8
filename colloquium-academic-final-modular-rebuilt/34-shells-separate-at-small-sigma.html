What training sees of $X_\sigma$

Fixed-$\sigma$ supervision heuristic $$Y=X+\sigma Z,\qquad \mathcal U_\sigma:=\bigcup_{i=1}^N \mathrm{Sh}_\sigma(x_i),\qquad C_\sigma(p,\mathcal D):=\mathbb P(Y\in \mathcal U_\sigma).$$ $$\mathcal L_\sigma(\theta)=\mathbb E\|m^\theta_\sigma(Y)-X\|^2 \approx \mathbb E\!\left[\|m^\theta_\sigma(Y)-X\|^2\,\mathbf 1_{\{Y\in \mathcal U_\sigma\}}\right].$$ At small $\sigma$, the supervised region is only a thin disconnected union of shells around the training atoms.
This slide is intentionally almost all visual. The slider changes sigma so the audience can see shells move from separated to overlapping. Keep the talk track on concentration and shell coverage.
