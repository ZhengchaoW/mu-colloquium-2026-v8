The major player is the denoiser

The denoiser appears naturally $$u_t(y)=\frac{\dot{\beta}_t}{\beta_t}\,y+\frac{\dot{\alpha}_t\beta_t-\alpha_t\dot{\beta}_t}{\beta_t}\,m_t(y),\quad m_t(y)=\E[X\mid X_t=y]$$ So once $m_t$ is known, the vector field $u_t$ is known too.
CFM loss reduces to denoiser regression
$$L_{\mathrm{CFM}}=\E_{t,X,Z}\bigl\|u_\theta(t,X_t)-(\dot{\alpha}_t X+\dot{\beta}_t Z)\bigr\|^2,\quad X_t=\alpha_t X+\beta_t Z$$
$$\dot{\alpha}_t X+\dot{\beta}_t Z=\frac{\dot{\beta}_t}{\beta_t}X_t+\frac{\dot{\alpha}_t\beta_t-\alpha_t\dot{\beta}_t}{\beta_t}X$$
$$u_\theta(t,y)=\frac{\dot{\beta}_t}{\beta_t}\,y+\frac{\dot{\alpha}_t\beta_t-\alpha_t\dot{\beta}_t}{\beta_t}\,m_t^\theta(y)$$
$$\arg\min_{u_\theta} L_{\mathrm{CFM}}=\arg\min_{m_t^\theta}\;\E_{t,X,Z}\bigl\|m_t^\theta(\alpha_tX+\beta_tZ)-X\bigr\|^2$$

Training, vector fields, and sampling all reduce to the denoiser $m_t$.

The animation reveals the four lines with staggered delays. The punchline is that the CFM loss, despite its velocity-field framing, is equivalent to a regression loss on the posterior mean $m_t$.
