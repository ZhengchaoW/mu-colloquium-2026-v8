Zoom in: the discrete case

In practice, all we actually observe is a finite collection of samples $\{x_1,\ldots,x_N\}$.

Empirical distribution

We package those observations into

$$p \;=\; \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N \delta_{x_i}.$$

So $\mathrm{supp}(p)=\{x_1,\ldots,x_N\}$ is exactly the finite training set.

Terminal theory

The previous slide gives the flow map

$$(\Psi_1)_\# \gamma = p,$$

so each initial noise sample is sent onto one atom of the empirical distribution: one training image.

This is the conceptual pivot: in practice the target is empirical, not an inaccessible ground-truth distribution. The terminal theorem already says the exact decoder lands on the empirical support. Next slide: why the denoiser formula makes that inevitable.
