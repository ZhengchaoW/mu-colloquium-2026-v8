Quantifying memorization per step

What we measure
$$g = m^\theta_\sigma(x_{\mathrm{test}}+\sigma Z).$$

We call this one-step output memorized when it lands clearly closer to one training image than to the runner-up:

$$d_{1\mathrm{NN}}(g) < \frac{d_{2\mathrm{NN}}(g)}{3}.$$

This is the paper’s exact per-noise criterion.

Now make the per-noise question precise. We are not asking whether the full trajectory memorizes yet; we are asking whether one denoising step from a held-out test point has already snapped to a training image. This is the exact rule used in the paper.
