memorization: interaction of two players

Player 1: posterior concentration $$w_i(y,\sigma)=\frac{\exp\!\left(-\|y-x_i\|^2/(2\sigma^2)\right)}{\sum_{j=1}^N \exp\!\left(-\|y-x_j\|^2/(2\sigma^2)\right)}$$ $$W_\sigma(\mathcal D):=\mathbb E\!\left[\max_i w_i(X+\sigma Z,\sigma)\right]$$ High $W_\sigma$ means the empirical-optimal denoiser is already dominated by one training image.
Player 2: shell coverage $$\mathcal U_\sigma:=\bigcup_{i=1}^N \mathrm{Sh}_\sigma(x_i)$$ $$C_\sigma(p,\mathcal D):=\mathbb P(X+\sigma Z\in \mathcal U_\sigma)$$ High $C_\sigma$ means noisy test samples actually land in regions that training has supervised.
Pedagogical summary Memorization needs both players. Small noise lacks coverage; large noise lacks concentration; the danger zone is the intermediate overlap.
This is the new perspective slide. Do not rush it. The whole point is to make the audience see memorization as the interaction of two quantities, not as a one-number story about sigma.
